Thursday, July 17: Like God to Pharaoh

Today's focus: How the Lord deals with Moses’ objection as he prepares to lead God's people.

Moses' Mission and Objection

The Lord commands Moses to go and speak with Pharaoh. However, Moses expresses his lack of self-confidence and objects:

Exodus 6:28-30 (KJV):
28 And it came to pass on the day when the LORD spake unto Moses in the land of Egypt,
29 That the LORD spake unto Moses, saying, I am the LORD: speak thou unto Pharaoh king of Egypt all that I say unto thee.
30 And Moses said before the LORD, Behold, I am of uncircumcised lips, and how shall Pharaoh hearken unto me?

Understanding Moses' Deeper Objection

The lesson commentary provides additional insight into Moses' objection:

"With a lack of self-confidence, Moses again objects, 'Why would Pharaoh listen to me?' Here again we can see not just Moses’ humility but, again, his desire to get out of the task, which so far has not gone too well."

According to this lesson commentary, what was a significant underlying factor in Moses' objection, beyond his lack of self-confidence?

The Lord's Response to Moses' Objection

Now, considering all of Moses' reasons for objecting, how does the Lord deal with Moses’ objection, particularly regarding his speaking ability?

Aaron: Moses' Prophet & The Role of a Prophet

God mercifully gives Aaron to help Moses, defining the role of a prophet:

Exodus 7:1-2 (KJV):
1 And the LORD said unto Moses, See, I have made thee a god to Pharaoh: and Aaron thy brother shall be thy prophet.
2 Thou shalt speak all that I command thee: and Aaron thy brother shall speak unto Pharaoh, that he send the children of Israel out of his land.

A prophet is a spokesperson for God; he or she is His mouthpiece to transmit and interpret God’s word to the people.

Pharaoh's Hardened Heart and God's Purpose

God warns Moses that the confrontation will be tense and long, and that Pharaoh's heart will be hardened:

Exodus 4:21 (KJV):
And the LORD said unto Moses, When thou goest to return into Egypt, see that thou do all those wonders before Pharaoh, which I have put in thine hand: but I will harden his heart, that he shall not let the people go.

Exodus 7:3 (KJV):
And I will harden Pharaoh's heart, and multiply my signs and my wonders in the land of Egypt.

Question: The Ultimate Outcome

Despite the resistance and the hardening of Pharaoh's heart, what is the ultimate positive outcome God declares will result?

Exodus 7:5 (KJV):
And the Egyptians shall know that I am the LORD, when I stretch forth mine hand upon Egypt, and bring out the children of Israel from among them.

Concluding Thought

Just as God dealt with Moses' objections and empowered him for the task, He will also equip us and glorify Himself through our obedience, even when things seem **"rough."**
